# L01 — Introduction to DevOps, SRE & Platform Engineering

## Overview

This is the first lecture. It sets the mental model for **everything** that follows. Most engineers skim introductions and pay for it later by confusing DevOps with "doing CI/CD" or "running Kubernetes". By the end of this lecture, you will understand DevOps as a cultural, organizational, and technical movement — and you will be able to articulate clearly how DevOps, SRE, and Platform Engineering relate, overlap, and differ.

This lecture has **6 chapters and 28 topics**.

## Learning Outcomes

After completing this lecture, you will be able to:

1. Define DevOps precisely and defend the definition against common misconceptions
2. Trace the historical evolution from Waterfall → Agile → DevOps → SRE → Platform Engineering
3. Explain the CALMS framework and Three Ways with concrete examples
4. Compare and contrast DevOps Engineer, SRE, Platform Engineer, and Cloud Engineer roles at FAANGM companies
5. Compute and interpret all four DORA metrics
6. Identify common anti-patterns in DevOps adoption and articulate why they fail

## Prerequisites

- Basic understanding of what software is and how it gets to production (at any level)
- A high-level idea of what "operations" means in IT

## Assessment

By the end of this lecture, you should be able to confidently answer:

- "What is DevOps?" — in 30 seconds, 5 minutes, and 30 minutes
- "How do you measure DevOps success?" — with numbers
- "Why does Google call it SRE and not DevOps?" — with history
- "What's wrong with creating a DevOps team?" — with logic
- "What DORA metrics does your team have, and what do they tell you?" — with rigor
