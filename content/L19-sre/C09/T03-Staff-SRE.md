# L19/C09/T03 — The Staff SRE Role

## Learning Objectives

- Understand Staff/Principal SRE
- Operate at scale

## Levels

Typical:
- Junior SRE (E2)
- SRE (E3-E4)
- Senior SRE (E5)
- Staff SRE (E6)
- Sr Staff SRE (E7)
- Principal SRE (E8+)
- Distinguished (E9+)

## Staff SRE

Scope:
- Multiple services / teams
- Cross-functional projects
- Strategy contributions
- Mentor

## Key Activities

### Architecture
Design org-wide:
- Multi-region
- Disaster recovery
- Platform

### Strategy
Quarterly + yearly:
- Reliability roadmap
- Investment areas
- Headcount

### Mentoring
Junior + mid SREs:
- Pair
- Review
- Career growth

### Cross-Team
Coordinate:
- Multiple teams
- Org-wide standards

### Crisis
Escalation point:
- Big incidents
- IC for major
- Postmortem leadership

## What Staff Doesn't Do

- Pure tickets (delegate)
- Single service ownership
- Tactical work mostly

For: strategic.

## Time Allocation

```
Strategy: 30%
Design / Architecture: 25%
Code: 15%
Mentoring: 15%
Meetings / coord: 10%
Crisis: 5%
```

## What "Senior" Means

- Operates independently
- Multi-faceted projects
- Coaches others
- Tech voice in team

## What "Staff" Means

- Operates across teams
- Owns initiatives
- Strategy contributions
- Tech voice in org

## What "Principal" Means

- Operates across org
- Long-term strategy
- External voice
- Recognized authority

## Visibility

- Internal: respected
- External (sometimes): blog, conferences

## Compensation

Staff: similar to senior PM, EM.

Path:
- IC
- Manager (different track)

## Promotion Criteria

For staff:
- Demonstrated org-wide impact
- Multiple teams influenced
- Strategic projects led

Hard. Years.

## Common Paths

- Strong IC who grew
- Senior eng who shifted to SRE
- Cross-domain experience

## Tools

Staff SRE uses:
- Calendar (much meeting)
- Whiteboard (design)
- Docs (specs, RFCs)
- Slack (orchestrate)
- Code (selectively)

## Less Coding

Some staff:
- Less coding
- More strategy

OK if impact high.

But: stay technical (don't drift to manager).

## Both Are Valid

- IC track: stay deep technical
- Manager track: lead people

Org should have both.

## Switching

IC → Manager often:
- People skills strong
- Tired of coding
- Want to grow team

Manager → IC less common but valid.

## Burnout

Risk:
- High expectations
- Always senior
- No "easy" tasks

Mitigate:
- Recovery
- Diverse projects
- Mentor (recharge)

## Manager Counterpart

Staff SRE + Senior Eng Manager:
- Together lead team
- Diff scope
- Complementary

For: ladder dual.

## Interview Prep

**Junior**: "What is a Staff SRE?" — A senior individual contributor (around E6) whose scope spans multiple services and teams — leading cross-functional projects, contributing to strategy, and mentoring — rather than owning a single service or working tickets.

**Mid**: "How does Senior differ from Staff and Principal?" — Senior operates independently on multi-faceted projects and is the tech voice within a team, Staff operates across teams and owns org-wide initiatives, and Principal sets long-term strategy across the whole org and is often an external voice and recognized authority.

**Senior**: "How does a Staff SRE spend their time?" — Roughly 30% strategy, 25% architecture and design, and the rest split across selective coding, mentoring, coordination, and crisis escalation — delegating tactical ticket work while staying technical enough not to drift into a pure-manager role.

**Staff**: "What does an org actually value at the Staff+ level, and how do you avoid the traps?" — Multi-team impact, strategic clarity, mentoring, crisis leadership, and technical vision — earned over years of demonstrated org-wide influence; the traps are doing all coding (not Staff work), all meetings (no technical grounding), or hero work without mentoring, and the mitigation for the constant-senior burnout risk is recovery, diverse projects, and recharging through mentoring.
