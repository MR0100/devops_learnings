# L29/C04/T01 — STAR Format

## Learning Objectives

- Use STAR
- Tell strong stories

## STAR

- **S**ituation
- **T**ask
- **A**ction
- **R**esult

For: structured answers.

## Why

- Concise
- Specific
- Memorable
- Evaluable

## Example

Q: "Tell me about a time you led a critical project."

A:
- S: "Last year, payments service had 99.5% uptime; below 99.9% SLO."
- T: "I owned bringing it to 99.95%."
- A: "Led 4-person team. Profiled hot paths. Migrated to async queue for downstream calls. Added circuit breakers. Set up better alerts."
- R: "Reached 99.97% in 3 months. Reduced p99 by 40%. Mentored 2 junior engineers."

## Pre-Prep Stories

Have 10-15 stories ready:
- Technical
- Leadership
- Conflict
- Failure
- Growth

For: any behavioral.

## Story Bank

```
1. Led migration to K8s
2. Reduced incident MTTR
3. Mentored junior; growth
4. Handled disagreement with PM
5. Failed deploy; learned
6. Drove cross-team project
7. Influenced architecture
8. Improved on-call
9. Hired team
10. Product impact
```

Tag per LP / competency.

## Action Detail

Spend most time on Action:
- What you did
- Specifically
- "I" not "we"

For: your contribution clear.

## Quantify Result

- "Increased X by 40%"
- "Saved $200k/year"
- "Reduced MTTR from 30 to 5 min"

For: concrete.

## Avoid

### "We" Only
"We did X." Your role?

### Vague
"Improved a lot."

### No Result
Story without outcome.

### Long
Trim to 2-3 minutes.

## Practice

- Out loud
- Time
- Get feedback

## Follow-Up

Interviewer:
- "What did you learn?"
- "What would you do differently?"
- "Who else was involved?"

Prep for:
- Self-reflection
- Growth mindset

## Best Practices

- STAR structure
- Specific
- I-focused
- Quantified
- Concise

## Common Mistakes

- "We"
- No result
- Long-winded
- Vague

## Quick Refs

```
S: where
T: your role
A: what you did (most time)
R: outcome (quantified)

10-15 stories prepped
Tagged per competency
```
