## Amazon

### Roles
- SDE (Software Development Engineer) — most common
- AWS Cloud Support Engineer
- Solutions Architect
- TPM (Technical Program Manager)
- DevOps Engineer (newer separate track at AWS)

### Levels
- L4 SDE I (new grad)
- L5 SDE II (3-5 yr)
- L6 SDE III (senior; 6-10 yr)
- L7 Principal SDE
- L8 Senior Principal
- L10 Distinguished Engineer
- L11 VP

For DevOps-flavored roles, L5-L7 is the senior range.

### Process
1. Recruiter screen (30 min)
2. Online assessment (sometimes; DSA + work simulation)
3. Phone screen (60 min: 1 coding + LP)
4. Onsite (5-6 rounds)
   - 2 coding
   - 1 system design (L6+)
   - 1-2 behavioral (LP-focused)
   - 1 bar raiser (skip-level + LP)
5. Debrief + offer

### Bar Raiser
- Trained interviewer from outside hiring team
- Veto power
- Calibrates against bar

### Leadership Principles (16)
- Customer Obsession
- Ownership
- Invent and Simplify
- Are Right, A Lot
- Learn and Be Curious
- Hire and Develop the Best
- Insist on the Highest Standards
- Think Big
- Bias for Action
- Frugality
- Earn Trust
- Dive Deep
- Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
- Deliver Results
- Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
- Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

### Preparation
- 2-3 stories per LP (use Career Story Bank)
- STAR format
- Specific, quantified, individual ("I", not "we")
- Practice OUT LOUD

### Critical LPs for DevOps
- Ownership
- Dive Deep (production debugging stories)
- Bias for Action
- Insist on Highest Standards
- Earn Trust
- Frugality

### Frugality Examples
- "We saved $X by..."
- "Instead of buying Y, we built..."
- "Reduced AWS bill by 40% via..."

## Google

### Roles
- SWE — software engineer (broad)
- SRE — site reliability engineer
- Production Engineer (subset)

### Levels
- L3 (new grad)
- L4 (3 yr)
- L5 Senior (5-8 yr)
- L6 Staff (8-15 yr)
- L7 Senior Staff (15+ yr)
- L8 Principal
- L9 Distinguished

### Process
1. Recruiter
2. Phone screen (60 min coding)
3. Onsite (4-5 rounds)
   - 2 coding (algorithmic; ~hard)
   - 1 system design (L5+)
   - 1 Googleyness & Leadership
   - 1 role-related (SRE-specific for SRE roles)
4. Hiring Committee
5. Team match

### Hiring Committee
- Reviews "packet" (interview feedback + resume)
- Not on team; not biased toward this role
- Decides hire/no-hire

### Googleyness & Leadership
- Curiosity (continuous learning)
- Comfort with ambiguity
- Collaboration
- User-focused

### General Cognitive Ability (GCA)
- Problem decomposition
- Reasoning under ambiguity
- Asking good questions
- Open-ended problem solving

Often tested in: behavioral + open system design.

### SRE-Specific
- Linux internals (deep)
- Networking (TCP, DNS, HTTPS, routing)
- Distributed systems (consensus, replication, CAP)
- Reliability practices (SLI/SLO, error budgets)
- Capacity planning
- Incident response

## Meta

### Roles
- Software Engineer (E-track)
- Production Engineer (PE — Meta's SRE equivalent)
- Network Engineer

### Levels
- E3 (new grad)
- E4 (3-5 yr)
- E5 Senior (5-10 yr)
- E6 Staff (10-15 yr)
- E7 Principal (15+ yr)

### Process
1. Recruiter
2. Phone screen (45 min coding + 15 min behavioral)
3. Onsite (5 rounds)
   - 2 coding
   - 1 system design
   - 1 behavioral (E5+)
   - 1 product design or technical depth (varies)
4. Calibration

### Behavioral / Leadership Pillars
- Engineering excellence
- Impact
- Direction (for E5+)
- People (mentorship)

### Move Fast
- Bias to action
- Ship + iterate
- "Move fast and break things" (mellowed; now "move fast with stable infra")

### Common Behavioral
- Disagreement with manager / peer
- Hard project that succeeded / failed
- Most impactful work
- Mentorship

## Netflix

### Roles
- Senior Software Engineer
- Staff Software Engineer
- Reliability Engineer (rare; mostly senior SWEs do reliability)

### Levels (flat)
- Senior SWE
- Staff SWE
- Senior Staff SWE
- Principal SWE

### Process
1. Recruiter
2. Phone screen (60 min)
3. Onsite (4-5 rounds)
   - Coding (less algorithmic; more systems)
   - System design
   - Behavioral
   - Culture interview (Netflix Culture Memo)

### Culture
- "Freedom and Responsibility"
- High performance (Keeper Test)
- Adults treated as adults
- Documented in Culture Memo (read it!)

### Keeper Test
> "Which of my people would I fight to keep?"

If "wouldn't fight" — generous severance. Strong culture of high bar.

### Compensation
- Among highest in industry
- Flat structure ("market level top")
- Few RSUs; mostly cash

### Behavioral
- Real-world tradeoffs
- Adult judgment
- Strong opinions, well-defended

## Tailoring Prep

For each company:
- Read their engineering blog
- Read their culture memo (Netflix), LPs (Amazon), Pillars (Meta)
- Recent technical bets (open source, articles)
- Connect with current employees
- Practice tailored stories

## Common Themes Across All

- Leadership (without authority)
- Conflict resolution
- Failure + learning
- Long-term technical bets
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Hiring / mentoring

These work everywhere. Prepare these well; tailor with company-specific framing.

## Interview Themes

- "Tell me about Amazon's LPs"
- "How does Google's Hiring Committee work?"
- "Netflix Keeper Test — explain"
- "Why Microsoft growth mindset matters"
- "Meta E5 vs Google L5 — calibrate"
