# L29/C06 — Negotiation

## Topics

- **T01 Compensation Components** — Base, bonus, RSU, sign-on, refresher (annual stock).
- **T02 Level Negotiation** — Sometimes scope justifies a higher level. Push when warranted.
- **T03 Competing Offers** — The #1 leverage. Always have one if possible.
- **T04 levels.fyi & TeamBlind Realism** — Data is power. Use it.

## Comp Components

```
Total Comp Year 1 = Base + Sign-on/1 + RSUs/N + Target Bonus

RSU vesting:
- 4-year vests; cliff varies
- Front-loaded (25/25/25/25) — common
- Back-loaded (5/15/40/40) — Amazon style; sign-on covers Y1/Y2

Stock prices can move ±50% — calc TC at current price + assumed appreciation/decay
```

## Negotiation Principles

1. **Never accept the first offer.** Recruiters expect negotiation.
2. **Express enthusiasm + ask for time.** "I'm very excited; can I take a couple days to evaluate?"
3. **Use competing offers.** "I have another offer at $X total. To make this an easy decision, can you match or exceed?"
4. **Negotiate components separately.** Sometimes base is capped but RSU isn't. Sign-on is often most flexible.
5. **Get specific.** Don't say "more"; say "$30K more base + $80K sign-on + $200K additional RSU".
6. **Be friendly.** This becomes your manager/team. Adversarial = bad.
7. **Levels are negotiable** at the edges. If you're between L5 and L6 scope, ask for L6.

## When You Don't Have a Competing Offer

Options:
- Use market data ("levels.fyi shows L6 DevOps median TC of $X; your offer is $Y below")
- Use timing ("I need to make a decision by Z; can we improve the offer?")
- Use specific skills ("My K8s + Terraform + Go combo is rare; I think the market would price this higher")

## Real Negotiation Conversation

```
You: "Thank you so much for the offer. I'm very excited about [team / project / impact].

       I wanted to share some context: based on levels.fyi data for L6 DevOps roles at
       comparable companies, total comp is typically $750K-$900K. The offer is at
       the low end. I also have a verbal offer from [Company] at $820K.

       To make this an easy decision, can we improve the base to $X, sign-on to $Y,
       and stock grant to $Z over 4 years? Total TC about $820K Y1."

Recruiter: "Let me see what I can do."

You: (later, after counter)
"I appreciate the improvement. Can we close the gap on sign-on so the Y1 number lands at $810K?
 At that level I'll sign today."
```

## The "Day 1 Pay" Mindset

Don't undervalue:
- Sign-on (cash, taxed but immediate)
- Y1 RSU vest (often back-loaded)
- Annual refreshers (added stock granted each year of employment)
- Bonus target (% of base)

Don't overvalue:
- Hypothetical 4-year totals (price varies)
- "Career growth" promises
- Unspecified "future opportunity"

## Manage Multiple Offers

Time things:
- Get the first offer
- Use it as leverage with company #2 and #3
- Stagger deadlines (asking for 1-2 week extensions is usually fine)
- Final round: pit them against each other respectfully

## Common Mistakes

- Sharing current comp before getting an offer
- Accepting first offer "to avoid being greedy"
- Negotiating after signing the offer letter
- Being aggressive or threatening
- Ignoring stock price drop risk (Meta -75% from 2021 to 2022, then recovered)

## Tools & Data

- **levels.fyi** — verified offers per company/level
- **Blind** — anonymous TC discussions
- **Glassdoor** — older, less reliable but a baseline
- **Pave / Carta** (if you can access) — internal benchmarks

## Interview Themes

- Practice the conversation out loud
- Have a "walk away" number before negotiating
- Know your target level and BATNA
- Don't apologize for negotiating
