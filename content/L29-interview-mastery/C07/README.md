## Why Mock Interviews

Reading prep books gets you ~50% there. Practicing in front of another human gets you the other 50%.

- Reveals bad habits (don't ask questions, jump to code)
- Practices time-bound pressure
- Calibrates difficulty
- Feedback you'd never give yourself

For senior+ roles, 5-10 mocks before real interviews is standard.

### Friends / Network
- Engineers in target roles
- Especially with FAANGM experience

### Coach (Premium)

- $$$

## Mock Cadence

### 6 weeks out
- 1 mock/week (build skill + identify gaps)

### 4 weeks out
- 2 mocks/week (frequency builds muscle memory)

### 1 week out
- 2-3 mocks (final calibration)

### Day before
- Rest. Don't burn out.

## Mock Format

### Per Round Type

Coding mock (60 min):
- 5 min intro
- 50 min on problem
- 5 min feedback

System design mock (60 min):
- 5 min intro
- 45 min problem
- 10 min feedback

Behavioral mock (45 min):
- 5 min intro
- 35 min behavioral stories
- 5 min feedback

### Record Yourself
With permission, record. Watch back:
- How often you say "um"
- Body language
- Filler words
- Talking through approach

You'll cringe; that's the point. Improve from there.

## How to Self-Score

After each mock, grade yourself:

### Communication
- [ ] Restated problem
- [ ] Asked clarifying questions
- [ ] Verbalized thinking
- [ ] Engaged interviewer in design choices
- [ ] Listened to hints

### Problem Solving
- [ ] Identified data structure / algorithm
- [ ] Considered edge cases
- [ ] Brute force first
- [ ] Optimized with rationale
- [ ] Considered tradeoffs

### Code Quality
- [ ] Clean variable names
- [ ] Right level of comments
- [ ] Modular (functions where useful)
- [ ] Bug-free first run
- [ ] Tested with examples

### Time Management
- [ ] Reached working solution in time
- [ ] Didn't rush coding before agreement
- [ ] Left time for analysis

### System Design Specific
- [ ] Clarified scope + scale
- [ ] Capacity math
- [ ] Drew architecture
- [ ] Discussed tradeoffs
- [ ] Covered reliability + operations

### Behavioral Specific
- [ ] STAR structure
- [ ] Specific (not generic)
- [ ] Quantified impact
- [ ] Said "I" not "we"
- [ ] Showed self-awareness

## Score Each: 1-5

```
1 = Did not do
2 = Attempted
3 = Met expectations
4 = Exceeded
5 = Exceptional
```

For senior+ at FAANGM, aim for 4 on each.

## Feedback Pattern

### From Mock Interviewer
Ask for specific feedback:
- "What's one thing I should do differently?"
- "Did my system design lack depth in any component?"
- "Did I explain my reasoning clearly?"

Avoid: "How was I?" (gets vague answer).

### Self-Review (within 24h)
Don't just play the mock back; write:
- 1 thing that went well
- 2 things to improve
- 1 specific action to take next time

## Categorize Mistakes

### Conceptual (didn't know the topic)
→ Study the topic

### Communication (knew it but didn't show)
→ Practice articulation

### Time Management (ran out of time)
→ Skip the deeper optimization at the start

### Anxiety (knew it but blanked)
→ More mocks; calm reps

## Soft Skills Practice

Behavioral mocks especially:
- Recording yourself telling stories
- Watching back for: clarity, time, "we" vs "I", energy
- Edit and re-tell until polished

A great behavioral story is RIVETING, not a list of facts.

## Final Week

- Light mocks; mostly review
- No new topics
- Sleep
- Eat well
- Logistics (zoom test, environment)

## Day of Interview

- Eat
- Hydrate
- Quick mock or LeetCode warm-up (15 min)
- Arrive (virtually) 5 min early
- Calm + ready

## After Interview

Don't dwell. Each interview teaches you:
- What questions came up?
- What I knew well; what surprised me?
- What I'd add to story bank
- Areas to brush up

Win or lose, you grow.

## Mocking Behavioral with FAANGM Recruiters

Some recruiters will do mock behavioral with you. Take advantage if offered.

## Mock Interview Anti-Patterns

- Doing mocks but not reviewing feedback
- Not recording / no self-grade
- Treating mocks as performance (not learning)
- Only mocking what you're good at
- Cancelling mocks "because not ready" (you're never fully ready)

## Interview Themes

- "Mock interview strategy"
- "How do you self-improve?"
- "Walk through a recent mock and what you learned"
