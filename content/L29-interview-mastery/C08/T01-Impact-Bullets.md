# L29/C08/T01 — Impact-Driven Bullets

## Learning Objectives

- Write resume bullets
- Show impact

## Bad Bullet

"Maintained Kubernetes clusters."

Why bad:
- No impact
- No scale
- No metrics

## Good Bullet

"Led migration of 50+ microservices to Kubernetes, reducing deployment time 80% (45min → 9min) and infrastructure cost $200k/year."

Why good:
- Specific (50+ services)
- Quantified (80% reduction)
- Cost impact ($200k)
- Action verb (Led)

## Pattern

```
Action verb + What + Scale + Quantified outcome + (Why mattered)
```

## Action Verbs

Strong:
- Led, Drove, Architected, Designed
- Reduced, Improved, Increased, Optimized
- Built, Migrated, Automated
- Mentored, Hired, Promoted

Avoid:
- Worked on, Helped, Was responsible for

## Quantify

- Cost: $X saved
- Time: Y% faster
- Scale: N users / services / requests
- Quality: 99.99% from 99.5%

For: every bullet.

## Examples

### Reliability
"Improved payments service availability from 99.5% to 99.97% (P99 latency: 800ms → 200ms), enabling 4× transaction growth."

### Cost
"Reduced AWS spend $1.2M/year by right-sizing 200 EC2 instances and migrating to Spot."

### Speed
"Architected GitOps deployment platform reducing prod deploys from days to 15 min for 30 engineering teams."

### Leadership
"Mentored 4 engineers; 2 promoted to Senior within 18 months."

## Show Senior

For senior+:
- Lead / architect / drive
- Cross-team
- Strategic

Not:
- Just doing tasks
- Sub-scope

## ATS-Friendly

- Keywords (K8s, Terraform, AWS, SRE, etc.)
- Standard formatting
- No fancy fonts / columns

(See T02.)

## Length

- One page (< 10 yr exp)
- Two page max
- Tight

## Best Practices

- Impact first
- Quantify
- Action verbs
- ATS-friendly

## Common Mistakes

- Tasks not impact
- No metrics
- Weak verbs
- Too long

## Quick Refs

```
Action + What + Scale + Outcome
Quantify everything
Strong verbs
One page (usually)
```

## Interview Prep

**Junior**: "What makes a strong resume bullet?" — The pattern Action verb + what + scale + quantified outcome. 'Maintained Kubernetes clusters' becomes 'Led migration of 50+ services to Kubernetes, cutting deploy time 80% (45→9 min) and infra cost $200K/year.' Every bullet needs a number; without one it reads as a task, not impact.

**Mid**: "How do you quantify work that feels unmeasurable?" — There's almost always a metric: cost saved, percent faster, scale (users/services/QPS), or reliability moved (99.5%→99.97%). Even 'reduced on-call pages 60%' or 'cut a 2-hour manual process to 15 minutes' works. If I genuinely can't attach a number, I pick a different accomplishment.

**Senior**: "How does a senior resume differ from a mid-level one?" — Verbs and scope. Senior bullets lead with Led, Architected, Drove and show cross-team impact and strategy, not task execution. 'Architected the GitOps platform 30 teams deploy through' signals level; 'wrote Terraform modules' signals mid. The bullets should make my target level obvious before anyone reads the title.

**Staff**: "Your resume gets ~6 seconds — how do you optimize it?" — Front-load impact: the biggest, most quantified, most strategic bullets first, since recruiters skim top-down. I keep it to one page under ten years' experience, cut anything that's a task rather than an outcome, and make sure the top three bullets alone convey scale, leadership, and a number — because that's all most readers actually process.
