# L29/C08/T03 — Recruiter Outreach

## Learning Objectives

- Get found
- Respond to outreach

## LinkedIn

- Profile: complete
- Keywords: same as resume
- Headline: clear
- Open to work (sometimes)

## Headline

Strong:
- "Staff Site Reliability Engineer | Kubernetes, AWS, Multi-Region"

Weak:
- "Software Engineer"

## Photo

Professional. Friendly.

## About

3-4 paragraphs:
- Current role
- Strengths
- Looking for

## Experience

Same as resume:
- Impact
- Quantify
- Tech stack

## Skills

Add many:
- LinkedIn algorithm uses
- Endorsements

## Recommendations

Ask former managers / colleagues.

## Activity

- Engage thoughtfully
- Comment on industry
- Share projects

For: visibility.

## Open to Work

```
LinkedIn → Open to Work → Visible to recruiters
```

For: signal interest privately.

## Recruiter Messages

Respond promptly:
- Thank
- Show interest (or not)
- Ask about role
- Don't oversell

## Standard Template

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out about [role]. I'd love to learn more. Could we schedule a 15-min call?

A few questions:
1. What's the team focus?
2. What's the level / comp range?
3. Timeline?

Looking forward to chatting.

[You]
```

## Cold Outreach

If you reach out:
- Personalized (mention something specific)
- Brief
- Clear ask

## Active vs Passive

### Active
Apply + LinkedIn outbound.

### Passive
Open to Work; recruiters find.

For: both.

## Best Practices

- Strong profile
- Open to Work (when looking)
- Respond fast
- Polite always

## Common Mistakes

- Profile empty
- No keywords
- Slow respond
- Lie

## Quick Refs

```
Profile: complete + keywords
Headline: clear level + skills
Open to Work: signal
Respond: 24 hr
```

## Interview Prep

**Junior**: "How do you make LinkedIn work for inbound recruiting?" — A complete profile with a clear headline that states my level and key skills ('Senior SRE | Kubernetes, AWS, Multi-Region'), the same keywords as my resume, and the 'Open to Work' signal set to recruiters-only when I'm searching. A generic 'Software Engineer' headline gets skipped in recruiter searches.

**Mid**: "A recruiter messages you about a role — how do you respond?" — Promptly and professionally, within a day: thank them, express interest, and ask the three qualifying questions — team focus, level and comp range, and timeline — before committing any time. Fast, polite responses keep me in the pipeline; ghosting or overselling both hurt.

**Senior**: "How does the headline and keyword strategy actually drive inbound?" — Recruiters search LinkedIn by keyword and seniority, so my headline and skills section need to match what they search for at my target level. Listing the specific stack and adding many relevant skills feeds the algorithm and surfaces me; thoughtful activity — commenting on industry posts, sharing projects — raises visibility further.

**Staff**: "What's your strategy for cold outreach to a hiring manager or recruiter?" — Personalized, brief, and with a clear ask: I reference something specific about their team or a recent post, state in two sentences the value I'd bring, and ask for a short call. At senior+ a targeted, researched message to the right person converts far better than mass applications, and it bypasses the ATS entirely by getting a human to look first.
