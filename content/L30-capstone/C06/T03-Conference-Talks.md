# L30/C06/T03 — Conference Talks / Lightning Talks

## Learning Objectives

- Use public speaking as a portfolio and personal-brand asset
- Build a path from local meetups to major CFPs
- Turn one talk into multiple compounding artifacts

## Why Talks Matter

For senior+ DevOps engineers, a recorded talk is proof of depth that a resume can't convey. It demonstrates you can explain hard systems clearly — the exact skill staff engineers are hired for. Talks also generate inbound: recruiters, hiring managers, and peers find you through them.

## Start Local

The path to a KubeCon keynote starts at a meetup.

- **Local meetups** — DevOps Days, the local Kubernetes meetup
- **Lightning talks** — ~15 minutes, much easier acceptance
- **Why local first** — lower stakes, faster acceptance, builds confidence and reusable material

## Go Bigger

Once you have a few talks under your belt, target larger venues:

- **KCD** (Kubernetes Community Days — regional CNCF events, a great stepping stone)
- **KubeCon** (CFP is highly competitive)
- **SREcon**
- **AWS re:Invent**
- **HashiConf, AnsibleFest**, and other vendor conferences

### Submission Strategy

- **Submit to multiple** CFPs — acceptance rates are low; play the numbers
- **Be specific** — a concrete topic from recent, real experience beats a survey
- **Avoid "Intro to X"** — reviewers have CFP fatigue for beginner talks
- **Lead with real-world insight** — the failure you recovered from, the number you moved

## Internal Talks Count

A talk at your own company counts as portfolio **if it's recorded**. Record internal tech talks, get permission to share a sanitized version, and you've created portfolio material from work you were already doing.

## Make One Talk Compound

A single talk should spawn several artifacts:

```
1 talk → slides on GitHub / Slideshare
       → a blog-post version of the same content
       → a cross-link from your CV and LinkedIn
       → a CFP idea for the next, deeper talk
```

This is the compounding loop: a blog post seeds a CFP; a talk becomes a blog post; a GitHub project fuels both. Three years of consistent output builds a portfolio that opens doors.

## Post-Talk Checklist

- Push slides to GitHub or Slideshare (public, linkable)
- Write the blog version while the material is fresh
- Cross-link from CV, LinkedIn, and your personal site
- Capture the recording link; add it to your profile README

## Structuring the Talk

A reliable arc for a 15–30 minute technical talk:

```
1. Hook        (1 min)  — the problem / the outage / the surprising number
2. Context     (2 min)  — your environment, scale, constraints
3. The Meat   (8-15 min)— what you did, why, the trade-offs, what broke
4. Results     (2 min)  — concrete before/after numbers
5. Lessons     (2 min)  — what you'd do differently
6. CTA / Q&A   (3 min)  — repo link, slides, "find me after"
```

- **One idea per slide.** If a slide needs a paragraph, it's two slides.
- **Architecture diagrams over bullet walls.** People remember the picture.
- **Numbers, not adjectives.** "p99 went 500ms → 80ms" beats "much faster."
- **Rehearse to time.** Going over is the most common reason talks land badly.
- **Demos are high-risk.** Record a backup video; live demos fail on conference Wi-Fi.

### Handling Nerves

- Know your first 60 seconds cold — momentum carries the rest
- A friendly face in the audience to anchor on
- "I don't know, but here's how I'd find out" is a perfectly strong answer in Q&A

## Common Mistakes

- Submitting only "Intro to X" talks (CFP fatigue; low acceptance)
- Waiting for KubeCon instead of building reps at local meetups first
- Giving an internal talk and never recording it (lost portfolio value)
- One-and-done — not turning the talk into slides, a post, and the next CFP
- Generic content with no concrete numbers or real stories

## Best Practices

- Start at local meetups with lightning talks; build confidence and material
- Submit specific, experience-driven topics to multiple CFPs
- Record everything, including internal talks
- Turn every talk into slides + a blog post + a CV cross-link
- Pick 1–2 topics to be known for and go deep, talk after talk

## Quick Refs

```
Ladder: local meetup (lightning) → KCD → KubeCon / SREcon / re:Invent
CFP: submit to many, be specific, avoid "Intro to X", show real insight
Compound: talk → slides → blog post → next CFP idea → CV cross-link
Internal talks count — if recorded.
```

## Interview Prep

**Junior**: "Why give conference talks?" — They're proof of depth and communication skill, and they build your professional network and inbound opportunities.

**Mid**: "How do you get started speaking?" — Local meetups with short lightning talks; lower stakes, easier acceptance, and you build reusable material and confidence.

**Senior**: "How do you get accepted to a competitive CFP like KubeCon?" — Submit specific, real-world-insight topics (not "intro to X") to multiple conferences, draw on recent production experience, and lead with concrete outcomes.

**Staff**: "How does speaking fit a long-term personal-brand strategy?" — Pick 1–2 topics to own, go deep across multiple talks, and run the compounding loop — each talk becomes slides, a blog post, and the seed of the next CFP — so three years of output builds a recognizable portfolio.
