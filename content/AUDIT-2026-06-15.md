Accurate but under-depth; "For: X" fragment prose instead of teaching. Bimodal in L25 (a few excellent files beside many skeletal ones).

- **L28**: C04 design files thinner than the README; assert numbers instead of deriving.

- **Course-wide style**: ~476 telegraphic "For: X" fragments (heaviest in L18-L20, L25-L27).

## TIER 4 — Coverage gaps (missing topics worth adding)

- **L21**: **No transaction-isolation-levels topic** (READ COMMITTED vs REPEATABLE READ vs SERIALIZABLE, anomalies, PG-vs-InnoDB defaults) — biggest single gap; core interview material. Also no dedicated **indexing** topic.
- **L08**: **No ELB/ALB/NLB topic**; **no EFS/FSx**; DR strategies not laid out as the canonical 4; AWS Backup/SSM Session Manager absent.

- **L28**: Missing classic designs — **rate limiter, distributed queue/scheduler, KV/cache store, notification system, distributed locking**.
- **L02**: No **IRQ/NUMA** topic (README promises soft/hard IRQ content); filesystems omit fsck/inode-exhaustion/TRIM.
- **L17**: **Exemplars** only in READMEs, never a topic; native histograms light; no Collector deployment-patterns topic.

- **L27**: No **split-brain/fencing/STONITH** or **failback** coverage.
