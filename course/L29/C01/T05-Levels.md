# L29/C01/T05 — Levels at Each Company

## Learning Objectives

- Understand company levels
- Aim for right one

## Common

### Mid (L3-L4)
- IC; not lead
- Sub-scope

### Senior (L5)
- Owns features
- Mentors
- Independent

### Staff (L6)
- Cross-team
- Strategic
- Influence

### Senior Staff / Principal (L7-L8)
- Org-wide
- Strategy
- Recognized

### Distinguished (L9+)
- Industry-level
- Rare

## Google

- L3: SWE
- L4: SWE II
- L5: Senior SWE
- L6: Staff SWE
- L7: Sr Staff SWE
- L8: Principal Eng
- L9: Distinguished Eng
- L10: Google Fellow

## Meta

## Amazon

- L4: SDE I
- L5: SDE II
- L6: Sr SDE

- L8: Sr Principal

## Apple

- ICT2: Eng

## Microsoft

- 59-60: SWE
- 61-62: Sr SWE
- 63-64: Principal
- 65+: Partner

## Netflix

- Flat:
- Senior SWE
- Sr Staff
- Principal

## Compensation

By level:
- L5 / Sr: $300-500k total
- L6 / Staff: $500-700k
- L7 / Sr Staff: $700-1M+
- L8 / Principal: $1M-2M+

Approximate; varies by location, company, year.

## Down-Level

If interview suggests:
- "We can offer L5 instead of L6"

Decide:
- Accept (foot in door)
- Reject (try later)
- Negotiate

## Match Level

Aim for:
- Current level (lateral)
- One level up (stretch)
- Two up: unusual

## DevOps-Specific

- SRE / DevOps follows SWE ladder usually
- Some companies: separate (Google SRE)

## Best Practices

- Research level expectations
- Self-assess
- Target appropriate
- Be ready to discuss

## Common Mistakes

- Aim too high (fail)
- Aim too low (waste)
- Don't know target
