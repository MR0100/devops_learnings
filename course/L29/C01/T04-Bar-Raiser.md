# L29/C01/T04 — Bar Raiser / Hiring Committee

## Learning Objectives

- Understand decision process
- Pass committee

## Bar Raiser (Amazon)

Independent interviewer:
- Cross-team
- Trained
- Veto power
- Maintains hiring bar

## Process

- All interviewers debrief
- Vote: hire / no hire
- Bar Raiser strong influence
- Hire only with consensus

## Hiring Committee (Google)

Independent group:
- Reviews packet
- Decides
- Not interviewers

For: bias reduction.

## Packet

Includes:
- All interviewer feedback
- Levels assessment
- Hiring manager support
- Bar Raiser opinion

## Decision Criteria

- Technical bar (per level)
- Cultural fit
- Future potential
- Inclusive

## Outcomes

- Hire
- No hire
- Down level
- Up level

## Down-Level

Sometimes:
- Meets lower level only
- Offer at lower
- Accept or decline

## Bar Raiser Q&A

Bar Raiser may interview:
- Tough behavioral
- Architectural depth
- Why you (specifically)

## Process Time

After onsite:
- 1-3 days debrief
- 1-2 weeks committee
- Then offer

## Variation

- Amazon: Bar Raiser strong
- Google: HC strong
- Meta: hiring manager strong
- Apple: team strong

## Common Mistakes

- Inconsistent rounds
- Weak in any round
- Forget Bar Raiser is strict
- No prep for behavioral

## Best Practices

- All rounds strong
- Consistent narrative
- Specific examples
- Show level

## Quick Refs

```
Amazon: Bar Raiser veto
Google: Hiring Committee
Meta / Apple: hiring manager

Decision: consensus + bar
```
