# L29/C04 — Behavioral / Leadership Interviews

## Topics

- **T01 STAR Format** — Situation, Task, Action, Result. Time-box each: 30s, 30s, 2-3 min, 30s.
- **T02 Amazon Leadership Principles** — 16 LPs. Expect 2-3 stories per principle.
- **T03 Google's Googleyness & Leadership** — GCA (General Cognitive Ability) + Leadership + Role-related + Googleyness.
- **T04 Meta's Pillars** — Engineering Excellence, Impact, Direction, People.
- **T05 Common Themes** — Ambiguity, Conflict, Influence, Failure, Tradeoffs.
- **T06 Crafting a "Career Story Bank"** — 12 stories covering all themes.

## STAR Story Skeleton

```
SITUATION (30s)
- Context: company, team, role, time
- Stakes: why this mattered

TASK (30s)
- What you were responsible for
- Constraints, deadline, ambiguity

ACTION (2-3 min) ← bulk of the story
- What YOU did, not "we"
- Specific decisions and tradeoffs
- Conversations, conflicts navigated
- Technical depth where appropriate

RESULT (30s)
- Quantified impact
- Lessons learned
- What you'd do differently
```

## What Strong vs Weak Sounds Like

### Weak
- "We improved the deployment pipeline."
- "I worked on Kubernetes migration."
- "Our team had a conflict and we resolved it."

### Strong
- "I led the migration of our 50-service estate from EC2 to EKS. The CTO sponsored it but middle managers were skeptical because the team had been burned by a previous K8s attempt. I started by mapping each service's traffic profile and migrating a low-risk service first — this 'paved road' built credibility. By Q3 we had 40 services migrated. The result was deploy time from 2h to 8min and infra cost down 40%. The lesson: technical migrations are 80% stakeholder management."

## Common Themes Across Companies

- **Ambiguity**: "Tell me about a time without a clear path"
- **Conflict**: "How did you handle disagreement?"
- **Influence**: "How did you get others to follow without authority?"
- **Failure**: "Tell me about a project that failed"
- **Tradeoff**: "Tell me about a hard technical decision"
- **Long-term thinking**: "What's a multi-year bet you made?"

## Red Flags Interviewers Watch For

- "We" overuse (signal: was this really you?)
- No specifics (signal: didn't really happen)
- Always-positive (signal: lack of self-awareness)
- Blames others (signal: weak ownership)
- Doesn't answer the question (signal: deflects)
- No metrics (signal: shallow)

## Interview Themes

- Practice out loud (record yourself, listen)
- Time-box: ~5 min per story
- Have a recovery move: "Would it help if I picked a different example?"
- Be ready for follow-ups: "What did your manager say?" / "What would you do differently?"
