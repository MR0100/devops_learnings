# L29/C06/T02 — Level Negotiation

## Learning Objectives

- Negotiate level
- When to push

## Why Negotiate Level

- Comp band per level
- Career trajectory
- Title

For: significant difference.

## When Possible

If:
- Strong interview signal
- Other offers at higher
- Scope mismatch

## When Hard

- Strong process; committee decided
- Down-leveled by committee

For: rare to overturn.

## Approach

```
"I appreciate the offer for Senior. Based on my experience with [X], I believe I'm operating at Staff. Can we revisit?"
```

Show:
- Specific examples
- Comparable in other companies
- Real impact

## Evidence

- Current scope
- Reports / influence
- Project leadership
- Strategic work

## Risk

- Re-interview required sometimes
- Could lose offer

For: weigh.

## Down-Level

Sometimes offered:
- Accept (foot in door; promote later)
- Reject (try other companies / later)

For: depends.

## Recovery After

Once leveled:
- Hard to change quickly
- Promote takes 1-2 years
- Job change easier for level jump

## Companies Vary

Amazon: rigid.
Meta: more flexible.
Google: rigid.

## Compete Across Companies

If multiple offers:
- Use leverage
- Match level

For: pick highest level + comp.

## Best Practices

- Quantify scope
- Justify level
- Be willing to walk
- Don't bluff

## Common Mistakes

- Bluff
- Vague justification
- Push too hard (lose offer)
- Accept without trying

## Quick Refs

```
Strong signal + comparable level:
  "I'm operating at Staff because..."

Specifics + impact
Be willing to walk
```
