# L19/C09/T02 — Influence Without Authority

## Learning Objectives

- Lead without direct authority
- Drive change

## The Challenge

Staff/Principal SREs:
- No direct reports
- Lead across teams
- Must convince

For: harder than managing.

## Sources of Influence

### Expertise
Knowledge of systems.

### Track Record
Past successes.

### Relationships
Trust over time.

### Data
Hard numbers.

### Vision
Compelling future.

## Tactics

### Empathy
Understand other team's constraints.

### Listening
Not just telling.

### Win-Win
Their goals + yours.

### Patience
Big changes take time.

### Document
Decisions persist.

### Coalition
Allies aligned.

## Drive Standards

For org-wide standard:
1. Pilot with friendly team
2. Demonstrate value
3. Document
4. Promote
5. Other teams adopt

For: gradual.

## Don't

### Mandate
"You must do X." Generates resistance.

### Blame
Lose trust.

### Hoard Knowledge
Become bottleneck; no scale.

### Ignore Pushback
Real concerns; address.

## Do

### Educate
Talks, blogs, docs.

### Demonstrate
Show value via examples.

### Enable
Build tools that make right path easy.

### Recognize
Credit others' wins.

### Listen
Real concerns inform.

## Examples

### Migrating to New Platform
- Pilot with friendly team
- Show metrics improvement
- Doc the migration
- Office hours support
- Other teams self-onboard

### Reduce Toil
- Identify top toils across teams
- Build automation
- Show before/after time
- Make available

### Better Postmortems
- Run good ones yourself
- Doc template
- Lead reviews
- Coach others
- Spread

## Real-Time

In incidents:
- Calm
- Helpful
- Don't grandstand

For: trust accrues.

## Cross-Team Conflicts

Two teams disagree:
- Hear both
- Find shared goal
- Propose
- Iterate

Mediate, don't pick.

## Leadership

Leadership ≠ authority.

Some best leaders: no direct reports.

For: build people skill.

## Coach

Mentor junior:
- Share knowledge
- Pair on issues
- Promote
- Sponsor

For: multiplier effect.

## Calendar

Sr/Staff SRE:
- 30% coding
- 30% reviewing
- 20% meetings
- 20% strategy

For: scale.

## Public Visibility

- Blog posts
- Conference talks
- Internal showcases

For: extend reach.

## Difficult Conversations

When team resists:
- Listen
- Acknowledge concerns
- Find common ground
- Iterate

For: progress.

## Building Trust

- Deliver promises
- Be reliable
- Admit mistakes
- Help others succeed

For: trust = currency.

## Career Path

Staff SRE → Principal:
- Influence broader
- Strategy
- Org design
- External voice

## Common Mistakes

- Authority claim
- Top-down
- Ignore opinions
- No tools (just demands)
- Burn out heroically

## Real Examples

### Charity Majors
Vocal industry voice; built Honeycomb on observability ideas.

### Liz Fong-Jones
Honeycomb advocate; influence via writing + speaking.

### Many staff SREs
Internal influence; not famous.

## Best Practices

- Build trust over time
- Data > opinions
- Enable > mandate
- Coalitions
- Patience
- Recognize others

## Common Mistakes

- "Should" without "could"
- Lecture
- Skip relationships
- Hero work alone
