# L19/C03/T04 — Healthy On-Call Practices

## Learning Objectives

- Sustain on-call long-term
- Avoid burnout

## Sustainable

If on-call destroys engineers: not sustainable.

Goals:
- < 25% time on-call
- < 2 pages/shift
- Recovery after
- Continuous improvement

## Page Volume Targets

### Per Shift (Week)
- 0-2 pages: healthy
- 3-5: investigate
- 5+: emergency intervention

### Per Year
- 50-100: healthy
- 200+: dysfunctional

If consistently high: invest in noise reduction.

## Recovery

After shift:
- Comp day off
- No urgent deadlines
- Easy work

For: rest.

## Compensation

- Pay differential (US)
- Comp time (EU)
- Career advancement weight

For: respect the burden.

## Schedule

Predictable:
- Know weeks in advance
- Can plan life
- Vacation considered

For: family + life.

## Transitions

End of shift:
- Hand-off doc
- Open incidents
- Recent context

For: continuity.

## Workload Caps

While on-call:
- No critical project deadlines
- Reduced 1:1s
- Fewer meetings

For: focus on incidents.

## Tools

- Mobile app (charged)
- Laptop nearby
- Stable network (or hotspot)
- VPN ready
- Credentials accessible

For: response readiness.

## Mental Health

- Therapy access
- EAP programs
- Sabbatical for high-load

For: support.

## Burnout Symptoms

- Cynicism
- Detachment
- Constant fatigue
- "It's just always broken"
- Quitting

For: early intervention.

## Manager Role

- Cap volume
- Hear complaints
- Push back if unsustainable
- Invest in fix
- Compensate

For: protection.

## Postmortems

After bad shift / page:
- Discuss
- Action items
- Improve

For: continuous.

## Wellness Check-ins

Post-shift:
- "How was your shift?"
- "What was tough?"
- "What can we improve?"

For: catch problems.

## Hand-Off

```
Outgoing: 
  Open: incident-1234 (mitigation ongoing)
  Recent: 3 pages this week
  Followups: postmortem due tomorrow
Incoming:
  Acknowledged
  Asked questions
```

For: smooth.

## On-Call Improvement

Treat as project:
- Top 5 noise sources
- Quarterly review
- Action items
- Track over time

For: improving over time.

## Severity Discipline

Don't page for:
- Warnings (ticket)
- Disk 85% (warn)
- Single transient

Reserve for:
- User-facing impact
- Multi-event
- Sustained

For: noise vs signal.

## Auto-Resolve

```
If alert resolves in 5 min auto:
  Don't page after window
```

For: transients filtered.

## Quiet Hours

Some teams:
- Severity rules different at night
- Warning → wait for morning

For: sustain.

## On-Call Salary

- Pay band considers on-call
- Bonus for difficult years
- Career growth credit

For: economic respect.

## Lateral Move

If on-call burnout:
- Move to non-on-call role
- Different service
- Step back

For: career sustainability.

## Sabbatical

After years:
- 1-3 month break
- Recharge
- Return refreshed

For: long-term retention.

## Sign of Health

- Engineers volunteer for on-call
- New hires excited (not afraid)
- Retention high
- Pages low

For: indicators.

## Sign of Issues

- People quit citing on-call
- Trade for any other shift
- "I can't do another year"
- High frequency complaints

For: act.

## Best Practices

- Cap volume
- Compensation
- Recovery
- Predictable schedule
- Tooling
- Manager support
- Wellness focus

## Common Mistakes

- 2-person rotation (death)
- No comp
- Frequent pages tolerated
- No recovery
- Manager dismissive

## Quick Refs

```
Target:
  < 25% time on-call
  < 2 pages/shift
  Comp post-shift
  Predictable schedule
  Mobile-ready

Burnout:
  Cap volume
  Talk to manager
  Improve alerts
  Take breaks
```
