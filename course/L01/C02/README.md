# L01/C02 — DevOps Culture & Principles

## Chapter Overview

Culture is the dimension that most often kills DevOps adoption. Tools can be bought; culture must be built. This chapter dives into the cultural frameworks that distinguish a DevOps organization from one that just has CI pipelines.

## Learning Outcomes

- Apply CALMS to evaluate an organization's DevOps maturity
- Map the Three Ways to concrete engineering practices
- Articulate why silos persist even when org charts say they shouldn't
- Lead a blameless postmortem
- Defend "you build it, you run it" against common objections (on-call burden, knowledge gaps)

## Key Concepts

- **CALMS**: Jez Humble's mnemonic — Culture, Automation, Lean, Measurement, Sharing
- **Three Ways**: Gene Kim's model — Flow (left to right), Feedback (right to left), Continuous Learning (vertical)
- **Conway's Law**: Org structure mirrors system architecture
- **Psychological safety**: Amy Edmondson's research; teams that can speak up perform better

## Interview Themes

- Conflict resolution between dev and ops
- Building shared on-call culture
- Running effective postmortems
- Influencing without authority
