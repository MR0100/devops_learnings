# L01/C06/T01 — "DevOps is a Tool / Team / Title"

## Learning Objectives

- Spot the three forms of category error
- Diagnose the root organizational cause of each
- Propose interventions

## The Three Errors

### Error 1: "DevOps is a Tool"
"We're doing DevOps; we use Jenkins."

Symptoms:
- Tool conferences attended; outcomes unchanged
- New tool budget approved; team incentives unchanged
- "Best tool" debates dominate strategy discussions

Root cause: leadership commodified a movement into a procurement decision.

Intervention: tie tool investments to *outcome metrics* (DORA), not capabilities.

### Error 2: "DevOps is a Team"
"Our DevOps team handles deployment."

Symptoms:
- "Pre-DevOps" Ops team renamed
- Dev still throws code over the wall, just now to a "DevOps" wall
- Bottleneck moved, not removed

Root cause: org chart change without changing incentives or responsibility.

Intervention: dissolve the team into stream-aligned + platform team structure (Team Topologies).

### Error 3: "DevOps is a Title"
"We're hiring a DevOps Engineer to do DevOps."

Symptoms:
- 1–2 engineers expected to do the work of 20
- "DevOps Engineer" job description = "Senior SysAdmin + CI"
- Cultural change not requested or expected of devs

Root cause: leadership scoped DevOps as an IC role rather than a cultural shift.

Intervention: hire the IC (you'll need ops capacity), but also fund culture/process work explicitly. Have the IC partner with engineering leadership on practices.

## Talking Points for Each Error

When you hear: *"We have a DevOps team."*
Reply: *"Tell me about the work they do that product engineers don't."*

When you hear: *"We're rolling out DevOps via [tool]."*
Reply: *"What metric will tell you it worked?"*

When you hear: *"We hired a DevOps Engineer."*
Reply: *"Will product engineers be on-call too?"*

These questions surface the underlying confusion without confrontation.

## Interview Prep

**Mid**: "What's wrong with calling DevOps a tool?"

**Senior**: "Your CTO says 'we just hired a Head of DevOps to drive our DevOps transformation.' What's your reaction?"
- Cautious. Hiring leadership for the change is fine; but ask: is it a *culture* mandate or a *team* mandate? Push for the former.

**Staff**: "Walk me through how you'd reframe a 'DevOps team' anti-pattern in an org of 200 engineers."
