# L01/C01 — Understanding DevOps

## Chapter Overview

DevOps is one of the most misunderstood words in software engineering. It's not a job title, a tool, or a team. This chapter establishes a precise mental model so that every later chapter has solid ground to build on.

## Learning Outcomes

After this chapter, you can:

- Recite a one-sentence definition of DevOps and defend it
- Place DevOps in historical context relative to Agile, ITIL, Lean
- Explain how DevOps differs from "Ops with automation"
- Compare DevOps with Agile, SRE, and Platform Engineering using a clear axis (culture vs practice vs role)
- Cite the DORA research as the empirical basis for DevOps claims

## Key Frameworks Introduced

- **CALMS** (Culture, Automation, Lean, Measurement, Sharing)
- **Three Ways** of DevOps (Flow, Feedback, Continuous Learning)
- **DORA Metrics** (Deployment Frequency, Lead Time, MTTR, Change Failure Rate)
